About

A bit about me

LL

I'm a rising senior at Basis International School Guangzhou (BIGZ). I'm involved in student leadership as president of the student council and our peer tutoring program, and I also help lead the Broadway Theatre Arts Club and Math Honor Society.

I'm interested in microeconomics and behavioral economics, particularly how individual firms affect local communities. My fieldwork has looked at learning spillover effects among students, and I'm currently exploring how AI is shaping consumer behavior, especially around intertemporal choice.

Outside of school, I run, sail, and enjoy discovering stories through travel, reading, and film. I'm a devoted fan of figure skating, musical theater, and stand-up comedy.

Focus

What I've been researching and building

My work splits between independent research and hands-on community leadership. In the classroom, I've run independent studies on how AI shapes consumer decision-making in tourism, how learning effects spill over between students in Tianhe District, and how Starbucks builds a "phygital" third place through its marketing. Outside the classroom, I've spent three years on Student Council and as a Prefect, and two years leading our peer tutoring program, roles that have taught me as much about listening as they have about leading.
